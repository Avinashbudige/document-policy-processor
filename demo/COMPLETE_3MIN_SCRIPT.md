## 🎯 Speaking Tips

### Pace
- **Natural conversational pace**: 145-150 words per minute
- **Not too fast**: Judges need to understand
- **Not too slow**: Keep it engaging
- **Pause at commas**: Natural breathing points
- **Pause between sections**: 1-2 seconds

### Tone
- **Enthusiastic but professional**: Show you're excited about your project
- **Clear enunciation**: Pronounce each word clearly
- **Vary your pitch**: Don't be monotone
- **Smile while speaking**: It comes through in your voice

### Emphasis
Emphasize these key words/phrases:
- "AI-powered"
- "instant, accurate"
- "ninety-four percent confidence"
- "no exclusions"
- "semantic understanding"
- "serverless, scalable"

---

## 📝 Pronunciation Guide

**Technical Terms**:
- **OCR**: Say "O-C-R" (spell it out)
- **API**: Say "A-P-I" (spell it out)
- **S3**: Say "S-three"
- **GPT-3.5**: Say "G-P-T three point five"
- **Embeddings**: em-BED-dings
- **Cosine similarity**: CO-sine sim-i-LAR-i-ty
- **Textract**: TEXT-tract

**Numbers**:
- **94%**: Say "ninety-four percent" (not "nine four")
- **78%**: Say "seventy-eight percent"

## 🎬 Action Cues

### When to Pause
- **[Pause 2 seconds]**: Full stop, let information sink in
- **[Pause 1 second]**: Brief pause between thoughts
- **After clicking**: Wait for UI to respond before continuing

### Mouse Movements
- **Move slowly**: Fast movements are hard to follow
- **Hover to highlight**: Use cursor to draw attention
- **Click deliberately**: Make it clear what you're clicking

### Typing
- **Type at readable speed**: Not too fast
- **Pause after typing**: Let viewers read what you typed

## 🔄 Practice Routine

### Before Recording

1. **Read through entire script 3 times**
   - First time: Silently, understand flow
   - Second time: Out loud, normal pace
   - Third time: With timer, aim for 2:50-3:00

2. **Practice difficult sections**
   - Processing explanation (technical terms)
   - Results display (longest section)
   - AWS services (list of services)

3. **Record practice run**
   - Use phone to record yourself
   - Listen back for:
     - Pace (too fast/slow?)
     - Clarity (mumbling?)
     - Enthusiasm (boring?)
     - Timing (under/over 3 minutes?)

### During Recording

1. **Have script visible**
   - Print it out OR
   - Display on second monitor OR
   - Use teleprompter app

2. **Take your time**
   - Don't rush
   - Pause between sections
   - Breathe naturally

3. **Record multiple takes**
   - Section 1: Record 2-3 times
   - Section 2: Record 2-3 times
   - Section 3: Record 2-3 times
   - Section 4: Record 3-4 times (most important!)
   - Section 5: Record 2-3 times
   - Section 6: Record 2-3 times

4. **Pick best takes**
   - Review each recording
   - Select clearest, most enthusiastic version
   - Use in final edit

## 🔄 If You Make a Mistake

**Don't worry! You can:**

1. **Pause and restart the section**
   - Stop speaking
   - Wait 3 seconds
   - Start the sentence again
   - Edit out the mistake later

2. **Record sections separately**
   - Record introduction separately
   - Record demo separately
   - Record conclusion separately
   - Combine in editing

3. **Do multiple takes**
   - Record each section 2-3 times
   - Pick the best version
   - Don't aim for perfection in one take
